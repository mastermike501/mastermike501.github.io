## Context

Paul is writing to the church in Ephesus from prison. In his closing exhortation, he reminds believers that the Christian life involves spiritual warfare and that God has provided everything we need to stand firm.

## Key Verses

> Finally, be strong in the Lord and in his mighty power. Put on the full armour of God, so that you can take your stand against the devil's schemes. — Ephesians 6:10-11 (NIV)

## The Armour

1. **Belt of Truth** — grounded in God's truth and living with integrity
2. **Breastplate of Righteousness** — the righteousness of Christ that guards our hearts
3. **Shoes of the Gospel of Peace** — readiness to share and stand on the gospel
4. **Shield of Faith** — quenching the flaming arrows of doubt and temptation
5. **Helmet of Salvation** — the assurance of salvation that protects our minds
6. **Sword of the Spirit** — the Word of God, our only offensive weapon

## Reflection

The armour is not something we manufacture — it is God's armour that He provides. Our part is to *put it on* daily through prayer, Scripture, and faith. Notice that Paul ends this passage with a call to prayer (v. 18), which is the means by which we take up the armour.
