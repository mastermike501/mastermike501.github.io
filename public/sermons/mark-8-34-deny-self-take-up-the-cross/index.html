Deny Self & Take Up the Cross

Mark 8:34

6 July 2025

The Call

“Whoever desires to come after Me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross, and follow Me. For whoever desires to save his life will lose it, but whoever loses his life for My sake and the gospel’s will save it.” — Mark 8:34–35 (NKJV)

Introduction

  • Jesus teaches His disciples He must suffer, be rejected, be killed, and rise after 3 days (v31)
  • Peter rebukes Jesus — well-meant but misguided
  • Jesus rebukes Peter: “Get behind Me, Satan! For you are not mindful of the things of God, but the things of men.”

A Striking Contrast

Peter was inspired by God to confess Jesus as the Christ, the Son of the Living God…

…yet just moments later became a hindrance to the Lord’s ministry.

The Invitation & the Cost

  • Christ called all who were around Him — disciples and crowd alike
  • “WHOEVER desires” — no one was left out
  • He compels no one; it is a free choice to follow

Two Conditions of Discipleship

  1. Deny yourself
  2. Take up your cross

This invitation comes with a cost. The disciple isn’t just here to “vibe” or be in it for a good time.

1. Deny Oneself

The Greek word “deny” means to affirm that one has no acquaintance or connection with a person.

To deny oneself is to surrender totally to Christ — my will replaced with the Master’s will.

“All to Jesus I surrender,
All to Him I freely give.”

What the Master wants is what I want. My surrender is total.

How Is Total Surrender Possible?

  • Grow in our understanding of who we are in Christ (identity)
  • Grow in our understanding of who Christ is to us (position)

Without both, we may fail to fully surrender to Him as Master and Lord.

The Willing Servant

“But if the servant plainly says, ‘I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free,’ then his master shall bring him to the judges… and his master shall pierce his ear with an awl; and he shall serve him forever.” — Exodus 21:5–6 (NKJV)

A servant who willingly binds himself to his master out of love.

Forget One’s Self & Interests

  • All my interests are subservient to my Master’s will
  • Everything my Master wants done, I do
  • Everything my Master loves, I love
  • Everything my Master despises, I despise

I am an extension of my heavenly Master while I am here on earth.

The Life Verse of a Disciple

“I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me; and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by faith in the Son of God, who loved me and gave Himself for me.” — Galatians 2:20 (NKJV)

2. Take Up One’s Cross

This is the first mention of a cross in the Lord’s teachings across all 3 synoptic gospels.

His hearers had no idea He would die by crucifixion — yet they knew the image of criminals carrying crosses.

A Critical Contrast

  • Criminals are compelled to carry their cross because of their crimes
  • Christ followers choose to carry their cross because they understand the cost of their cause

Expect Trials & Tribulations

Those who desire to come after Christ must suffer as He suffered.

“Beloved, do not think it strange concerning the fiery trial which is to try you, as though some strange thing happened to you; but rejoice to the extent that you partake of Christ’s sufferings, that when His glory is revealed, you may also be glad with exceeding joy.” — 1 Peter 4:12–13 (NKJV)

Is the Gospel Worth It?

  • So worth it that it is worth carrying a cross for
  • So true that it is worth suffering for
  • So life changing that it is worth dying for

Christ’s Example

“Let us run with endurance the race that lies before us, keeping our eyes on Jesus, the pioneer and perfecter of our faith. For the joy that lay before him, he endured the cross, despising the shame, and sat down at the right hand of the throne of God.” — Hebrews 12:1–3 (CSB)

Application

  1. How do we react to suffering? The same test can harden your heart or soften it — allowing God to refine you.
  2. The Lord often doesn’t tell us why we suffer. Ask instead: “Lord, what can I learn in this time of testing?”

The Cost Is High — But His Is Higher

“The Lord is on my side; I will not fear. What can man do to me?” — Psalm 118:6 (NKJV)

When we follow the Lord with surrendered hands and hearts, our good Lord and Master will care for us in every circumstance.
